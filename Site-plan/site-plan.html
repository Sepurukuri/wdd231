Site Plan — South Buenos Aires Youth Soccer Academy

A three-page informational site for youth soccer players, parents, and coaches.

1. Site Name

South Buenos Aires Youth Soccer Academy

Chosen because it clearly identifies the geographic focus (South Buenos Aires) and the audience (youth soccer players / families). The name is memorable and straightforward for local families seeking training and events.

2. Site Purpose

This website will introduce and provide information about a local youth soccer academy located in the southern region of Buenos Aires. The site will include details about the academy’s training programs, age groups, coaching staff, practice schedules, tournaments, and registration information. It will be a three-page informational website consisting of a Home page, a Programs page, and a Registration & Events page. The website will include images, event schedules, and dynamic content relevant to players and parents.

3. Scenarios (sample user questions)

  • “What age groups are available and what is the weekly practice schedule for each group?”
  • “How do I register my child and what documents or fees are required?”
  • “Who are the coaches and what certifications or experience do they have?”

4. Color Scheme

Two primary colors plus neutrals are chosen for branding, accessibility, and contrast:

  • Primary Blue — #003366 (used for headings, header background, nav, and emphasis)
  • Accent Yellow — #ffcc00 (used for call-to-action buttons, highlights, and links)
  • Background Light — #f7fafc (page background)
  • Body Text — #222222 (paragraphs and readable text)

5. Typography

Selected font family: Roboto (Google Fonts).

  • Headings: Roboto bold (700) — used for h1, h2, h3.
  • Body: Roboto regular (400) — used for paragraph text and lists.
  • UI / Buttons: Roboto medium (300–400) — used for CTAs and small labels.

The site-plan document itself uses Roboto for headings and body to show usage.

7. Additional Notes

  • Pages: Home, Programs, Registration & Events (three pages total).
  • Dynamic content: Events and member spotlights will use a local JSON file and JavaScript (fetch + async/await).
  • APIs: Weather widget on the Home page for local field conditions (OpenWeatherMap API).
  • Accessibility: Form labels, keyboard focus order, and color contrast will be tested with DevTools CSS Overview and Lighthouse.
